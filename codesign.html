MENDING AS CO-DESIGN

When it comes to mending, I like to think about it as a creative process. While we typically tend to try and hide when things are broken, or immediately throw them out, mending can be a way to push back against our idea of what a 'broken' or unusuable object or clothing item is.Visible Mending is a specific mending practice wherein one makes their 'mend' highly visible or otherwise decorative.

This is my personal favorite way to mend and darn my clothes because something as simple as choosing a bright blue thread to fix a tan sweater tells a story. When you participate in visible mending, you become a designer of your garment alongside its original makers - the factory workers and its designer, the moth that ate a hole through it, nail, loose thrad, and even with the rest of your wardrobe! Mending is a form of design and moreso, it can be a form of affection and care for both your own clothes and yourself.
