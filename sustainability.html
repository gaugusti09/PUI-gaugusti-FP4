SUSTAINABILITY

There's no unwinding the importance of sustainability when it comes to talking textiles. Due to the high demand for new clothes and fabric-made items constantly, there is an immense amount of waste that occurs within production & long after a garment's purchase. The main source of textiles in discarded waste is clothing -- In 2018, 11.3 million tons of textile waste ended up in landfills.

Mending Practices like patching and darning are great ways to prolong the lives of your clothing and keep them in use! What are those? I'm glad you asked! Darning is a sewing technique for repairing holes or worn fabric areas, it is the creation of a miniature weaving in a preexisting garment. Patching (and patchwork) which is much more well known, is the practice of placing another fabric piece over a hole and stitching it in place. Both of these are great ways to help reduce waste and also keep your clothes nice for longer.

BUT I'M NERVOUS TO START..?

That's okay! If you're not quite ready to pick up the sewing needle, there are a lot of other ways to reduce waste such as thrifting, clothes swaps, or making your own.
